20 Camera Capture Instructions for AI Dataset

Follow these guidelines when capturing images to ensure maximum accuracy and performance for the Dog Breed Identification AI model.

Capture the dog's entire body in the frame.
Ensure the face is clearly visible.
Keep both ears visible and unobstructed.
Make sure the tail is visible, if possible.
Take the photo at the dog's eye level.
Use natural daylight or bright, even lighting.
Avoid blurry photos by keeping the camera steady.
Use a plain or uncluttered background.
Keep only one dog in the frame.
Make sure the dog is standing naturally.
Avoid extreme close-up or far-away shots.
Keep the head, body, and legs fully visible.
Avoid objects, people, or furniture blocking the dog.
Do not use filters, beauty mode, or heavy editing.
Keep the camera lens clean before taking the photo.
Capture the dog from the front or a 45° side angle.
If possible, take multiple photos (front, side, full body).
Ensure the photo is high resolution.
Avoid strong shadows, glare, or overexposed lighting.
Wait until the dog is calm and still before capturing the image.
